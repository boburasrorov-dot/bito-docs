# Orders

<details>

<summary>Orders list</summary>

When entering the **CRM → Orders** section, a list of all orders created in the system is displayed.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (342).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (343).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Through this section, you can create, manage, and control orders received from customers.

An order is a request from a customer for a product or service, which is later converted into a sale.

</details>

<details>

<summary>Creating an order</summary>

To create a new order, press the **Create** button and proceed through the following steps:

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (344).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (345).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Basic information

• **Number** — order number\
• **Customer** — select the customer placing the order\
• **Status** — order status (new, in progress, etc.)\
• **Date** — order creation date\
• **Responsible person** — employee responsible for the order\
• **Intermediary** — select intermediary if available\
• **Delivery date** — product delivery date\
• **Delivery address** — customer address\
• **Currency** — currency type\
• **Comment** — additional information\
• **Upload file** — order-related documents

Products

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (346).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

• Add products or services to the order\
• For each product enter:\
  — quantity\
  — price\
  — warehouse

• The system automatically calculates:\
  — total quantity\
  — total price\
  — discounts and taxes



Additional expenses

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (347).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

* You can add additional expenses to the order\
  (e.g., delivery, service fees, etc.)
* Expenses affect the total price

**How it works**

• Customer places an order\
• Order is entered into the system\
• Products and prices are set\
• Order is finalized\
• Order is **converted to a sale**

**Important note**

• After finalizing the order, it can be converted into a sales document\
• Through this, the order becomes an actual sale and accounting begins

</details>

<details>

<summary>Orders list (kanban view)</summary>

In the orders section, orders can also be viewed in Kanban (column) format.

This view makes it easier to group orders by status and manage their process visually.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (349).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (350).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Main features**

• View orders in columns by status\
• Move orders from one column to another\
• Quickly manage each order status\
• Visual process control

**Kanban columns**

• **New** — newly created orders\
• **In progress** — orders currently being processed\
• **Completed** — finalized orders\
• **Cancelled** — cancelled orders

After the **In progress** stage, users can add stages as desired and name them

**Result**

• Clear visibility of the order process\
• Employee work control\
• Quick identification of problem stages\
• Simplified order management

</details>

<details>

<summary>Dashboard</summary>

In the CRM → Dashboard section, general statistics and analytical data on orders are displayed.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (351).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Through this section, business owners can track order status, dynamics, and efficiency in one place.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (353).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

At the top of the dashboard, key order indicators are displayed:

• **New orders** — number of new incoming orders\
• **Orders in progress** — orders currently being processed\
• **Closed orders** — successfully completed orders\
• **Cancelled** — cancelled orders\
• **Total order amount** — total value of all orders

Through this block, businesses can quickly assess the overall situation.



**Order dynamics**

This graph shows order growth or decline over time.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (354).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

• **Current period** — results in the current time range\
• **Previous period** — comparison with the previous period

Through filters, you can select:

• Order amount\
• Number of orders

Through this, businesses can:

• see whether sales are increasing or decreasing\
• compare with the previous period\
• identify trends



**Order funnel**

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (355).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Shows how many orders are at each stage:

• New\
• In progress\
• Completed

Additionally:

• percentage share of each stage is displayed\
• average completion time is shown

Through this:

• identify which stage has accumulated orders\
• assess process efficiency



**Most ordered products**

This block shows the best-selling products.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (356).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

• product name\
• number of times ordered\
• percentage share

Through this, businesses:

• know which products are in demand\
• manage assortment correctly



**Top ordering customers**

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (357).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This shows the most active customers:

• customer name\
• total order amount

Through this:

• identify the most important customers\
• develop a strategy for working with them individually



**Top selling employees**

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (358).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This block shows employee performance:

• employee name\
• number of orders\
• total amount\
• share in total sales (%)

Through this:

• identify top salespeople\
• assess team performance

</details>
