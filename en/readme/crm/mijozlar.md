<summary>Creating a customer</summary>

When the Create button is pressed in the CRM → Customers section, a new customer addition window opens.

Through this section, new customers are entered into the system and all information related to them is stored.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (320).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (328).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (327).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

When creating a customer, the following information is entered:

• **Image** — upload a customer image or logo.

• **Customer type** — select customer type (individual or legal entity).

• **Name** — enter the customer's name or organization name.

• **TIN** — if available, enter the tax identification number.

• **Gender** — select customer gender (for individuals).

• **Responsible person** — assign a responsible employee for this customer.

• **Loyalty card** — enter or scan the loyalty card linked to the customer.

• **Source** — specify where the customer came from (advertisement, referral, etc.).

• **Phone** — enter the customer's phone number. Multiple phones can be added.

• **Category** — select which category the customer belongs to.

• **Location** — enter the customer's address or location (can be marked on a map).

• **Supplier** — if the customer is a supplier, turn on this indicator.

• **Upload file** — attach documents related to the customer.

• **Comment** — write additional information or notes.

• **Send message** — turn on or off the automatic message sending function to the customer.

• **Status** — specify whether the customer is active or inactive.

Through the additional information section, more detailed information can be entered:

• **Last name** — customer's last name.\
• **Father's name** — customer's patronymic.\
• **Date of birth** — customer's date of birth.\
• **Address** — additional address information.\
• **Legal entity** — if available, legal information.\
• **Telegram** — customer's Telegram account.\
• **Email** — customer's email address.

After all information is entered, it is saved and the customer is added to the system.

As a result, all customer information is stored in a unified system, making the process of working with them more convenient.

### Mutual settlement report

The mutual settlement report section displays all money movements with the customer in full.

Here you can accurately track how much the customer owes you or how much they have paid.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (332).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Table information**

• **Time** — when the operation was performed\
• **Operation** — what happened (sale, payment, etc.)\
• **Comment** — additional information\
• **Debit** — amount paid by the customer\
• **Credit** — amount of product or service sold to the customer\
• **Balance** — balance after each operation

**How it works**

• You sell a product to the customer → amount is **written to credit** (debt increases)\
• Customer makes a payment → amount is **written to debit** (debt decreases)

Result:\
• **Balance** always shows the customer's current debt

**Example**

1. A sale of 1,000,000 soums was made to the customer\
   → Credit: 1,000,000\
   → Balance: -1,000,000 (customer owes)
2. Customer paid 400,000 soums\
   → Debit: 400,000\
   → Balance: -600,000

**Benefit**

• Accurate accounting with the customer\
• Debt monitoring\
• Checking each operation by history

**Important**

The mutual settlement report is automatically generated:\
• When a sale is made\
• When a payment is received\
• During returns or other financial movements



### Balance

The Balance section displays the customer's overall accounting status with you.

Here you can see how much the customer owes you or how much money you have received from them in advance.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (333).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Current balance**

• Current balance — the customer's current accounting status

The balance is understood as follows:\
• If positive — you owe the customer (there is an advance)\
• If negative — the customer owes you

**By organization**

If you have multiple branches or stores:

• A separate balance is shown for each organization\
• You can see in which branch the customer has a debt

**Total**

• Total (in base currency) — overall balance across all branches

This allows you to quickly see the overall accounting.

**Balance is automatically calculated:**

• When a product is sold → balance increases (customer becomes debtor)\
• When customer makes a payment → balance decreases\
• If there is a return → balance is recalculated

**Example**

1. Customer bought 1,000,000 soums worth of products\
   → Balance: -1,000,000
2. Customer paid 300,000 soums\
   → Balance: -700,000
3. Paid another 700,000\
   → Balance: 0 (account closed)

 **Additional features**

• Debt limit — you can set a maximum debt for the customer\
• Conversion — if there are different currencies, they can be converted

**Benefit**

• Monitor customer debts\
• Quickly see which customer owes how much\
• Simplify accounting

If balance is positive — you owe the customer\
If negative — customer owes you  



### Cashback

The Cashback section displays the accumulated bonus (cashback) for the customer and its history.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (334).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

These bonuses are usually calculated as refunded amounts to the customer during the sales process.

• **Current balance** — the current cashback amount available to the customer

This amount is considered a bonus that the customer can use for future purchases.

**Cashback history** - Here you can see all cashback given to the customer and their movements.

**In the table:**

• **Time** — date and time when cashback was given\
• **Operation** — where the cashback came from (which sale or operation)\
• **Debit** — cashback amount used\
• **Credit** — cashback amount added

**How it works**

• When the customer makes a sale — cashback is given to them\
• This amount is automatically added to their cashback balance\
• On the next purchase — the cashback can be used

**Example**

• Customer made a purchase of 1,000,000 soums\
• 5% cashback given → 50,000 soums\
• This amount goes to the cashback balance

On the next sale:\
• The customer can use these 50,000 soums

**Important**

For cashback to work:\
• Cashback settings must be enabled in the system\
• In Settings → Cashback section, percentages or rules should be defined



### Sales history

The Sales history section displays a list of all sales made with the customer.

Here you can see when, how much, and what type of sale the customer made.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (85).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Table information**

• **№** — serial number\
• **Sale number** — unique ID given to each sale\
• **Sale type** — sale or returned sale\
• **Sold at** — date and time when the sale was made\
• **Status** — sale status (completed or canceled)\
• **Amount due** — unpaid amount for this sale\
• **Total returned** — if there was a return, the returned amount

**How it works**

• Each sale automatically goes into this list\
• If a product is returned — it is shown separately as "returned"\
• Unpaid amounts are linked to the balance

**Example**

• A sale of 999,000 soums was made\
→ Amount due: 999,000

• If payment is made\
→ This amount decreases

• If there is a return\
→ It appears in the "Total returned" column

**Benefit**

• View customer's purchase history\
• Check which sale was made when\
• Monitor debts and returns



### Product sales

The Product sales section displays a list of all products purchased by the customer.

Here you can see which product, when, and through which sale the customer bought.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (335).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Table information**

• **№** — serial number\
• **Customer** — customer who purchased the product\
• **Products** — name of the sold product\
• **Category** — which category the product belongs to\
• **Unit of measure** — product measurement (piece, liter, kilogram, etc.)\
• **Sale number** — which sale the product belongs to\
• **Status** — sale status (completed or canceled)

**How it works**

• Products within each sale are entered into this list separately\
• If there are multiple products in one sale — each appears in a separate row\
• If a sale is canceled or returned — it is shown through the status

**Example**

• Customer bought "Apple juice"\
→ appears in the list with product name and category

• If they also bought another product\
→ it is also shown in a separate row

**Benefit**

• See what the customer buys most\
• Identify best-selling products\
• Analyze sales by product



### Invoice

The Invoice section displays a list of all invoices issued to the customer.

Here you can see official accounting for sales made to the customer.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (337).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Table information**

• **№** — serial number\
• **Number** — invoice number\
• **Date** — invoice creation date\
• **Organization** — which branch or store it was created in\
• **Type** —\
  — **Income** — money received from customer\
  — **Expense** — product or service given to customer\
• **Amount due** — total amount for the invoice\
• **Paid** — amount paid by the customer

**How it works**

• An invoice is created for each sale\
• The customer's debt is formed through the invoice\
• When the customer makes a payment — the "paid" amount increases

**Example**

• A sale of 999,000 soums was made\
→ Amount due: 999,000\
→ Paid: 0

• Customer made a payment\
→ Paid: 999,000\
→ Debt closed

**Types**

• **Income invoice** — payments received from customer\
• **Expense invoice** — products or services sold to customer

**Filter and export**

• Invoices can be filtered by date\
• Can be separated by organization (branch)\
• Data export option available

**Benefit**

• Conduct official accounting with customer\
• See exactly how much debt there is\
• Link payments and sales

### Reminders

The Reminders section is used to record important tasks and reminders related to the customer.

Through this, you can plan when to contact the customer, remind about debt, or schedule meetings.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (338).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Main features**

• You can add a reminder for the customer\
• You can set date and time\
• You can specify the responsible employee\
• You can write the reminder text

**How it works**

• A reminder is created\
• The reminder appears on the specified date and time\
• The employee performs the necessary work with the customer

**Reminder content**

• **Deadline** — date and time when the reminder should be completed\
• **Responsible person** — who should complete it\
• **Text (comment)** — what needs to be done or the reminder content

**Example**

• 17.03.2026, 16:00\
→ "Remind customer about debt"

• 20.03.2026, 10:00\
→ "Call about new product"



### Audit history

The Audit history section stores a history of all changes related to the customer.

Here you can see who, when, and what information was changed.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (339).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Main features**

• View all changes in customer data\
• Compare old and new values\
• Identify when and by whom the change was made

**How it works**

• Every time customer information is changed, the system records it\
• Changes are saved in history format\
• Each change shows old and new values

**Change content**

• **Change type** — what information changed (for example: address, phone, type)\
• **Old value** — information before the change\
• **New value** — information after the change\
• **Date and time** — when the change was made\
• **Status** — whether it was created or updated

**Example**

• Address was changed\
→ Old: not available\
→ New: Yunusabad, Tashkent

• Type was changed\
→ Old: none\
→ New: Individual

**Benefit**

• Monitor who changed what\
• Identify and check errors\
• Maintain data history



### Message history

The Message history section displays a list of all SMS or messages sent to the customer.

Through this, you can monitor what messages were exchanged with the customer and whether they were delivered.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (340).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Table information**

• **№** — serial number\
• **Phone number** — customer number where the message was sent\
• **Recipient name** — customer name\
• **Content** — text of the sent message\
• **Status** — message status (sent, delivered, error)\
• **Created at** — date and time when message was sent\
• **Reason** — reason for sending the message (for example: debt reminder, promotion, system notification)

**How it works**

• Every SMS sent through the system is stored here\
• When a message is sent, its status is recorded\
• If an SMS platform (for example Eskiz) is connected, the status is updated

**Example**

• "You have a debt of 150,000 soums"\
→ Status: Delivered

• "New product arrived"\
→ Status: Sent

**Benefit**

• Monitor messages sent to the customer\
• Check which message was delivered\
• See effectiveness of marketing and reminders
