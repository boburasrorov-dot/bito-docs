# Sales Reports

<details>

<summary>General Sales</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (214).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **General Sales** page is designed for overall analysis of sales indicators based on selected filters. All data on the page is automatically updated according to active filters.

The top section displays key financial indicators:

* **Sold** — total sales amount for the selected period and filter.
* **Returned** — total amount of returned products.
* **Discount** — total amount of discounts given.
* **Revenue** — net revenue, taking returns into account.
* **Gross Profit** — net profit (result after deducting cost).

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (216).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



**Chart**

Below the indicators is a chart (diagram) that visually represents the above data. Users can change the chart type:

* Column chart
* Line chart

<div><figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (217).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure> <figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (218).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure></div>

**Sales List**

The bottom section of the page presents a list of all sales in table format. The table is also formed according to active filters and enables detailed analysis.

This page serves to view sales volume, returns, discounts, and profit indicators in one place and make quick management decisions.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (219).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



</details>

<details>

<summary>Sales by Products</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (221).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Sales by Products** page is designed for analyzing sales indicators by product segment based on selected filters. All data is formed according to active filters.&#x20;

\
**Top Section**

On the left side of the page is the **Top 5 Products** block, showing the best-selling products and their sales amounts. This block enables quick viewing of product rankings.

On the right side is the **Product Sales Chart (diagram)**. The diagram visually shows sales dynamics by products. Each product is highlighted by different colors. The diagram updates based on filtered dates or other parameters.

(Visible only when a date is selected)

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (222).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



**Main Table**

At the bottom of the page is a detailed analysis table by products. This table enables viewing sales and profit indicators for each product in exact numbers.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (223).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

It helps determine product profitability. For example:

* Which product is selling the most,
* Which product is generating low profit or losses,
* Which products have high returns,
* How discounts and costs affect profit.

At the bottom of the table:

* **Total** — shows overall results for all products by selected filter. This enables quick assessment of overall sales and profit status.
* **Forecast** — estimated indicators calculated based on available data. This serves to assess and plan future results in advance.

</details>

<summary>Sales by Category</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (241).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Sales by Category** report displays sales results by product categories. This report enables analysis of which category is selling more and which category has lower sales.

The report table displays the following information:

* **Name** — product category name
* **Quantity Sold** — total number of products sold in this category
* **Quantity Returned** — number of returned products
* **Sales** — total sales amount by category
* **Returned** — amount of returned products
* **Discount** — amount of discounts given
* **Tax Included** — tax amount added to sales

If **"No Category"** is shown in the table, it means sales data for all products not assigned to a category are aggregated in this row.

At the bottom of the table:

* **Total** — overall results by selected filters
* **Forecast** — forecast indicators calculated by the system

This report helps the business identify which product categories generate the main revenue.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (242).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



</details>

<details>

<summary>Product Sales</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (226).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Product Sales Report** page shows each sales operation in detail by product. This report clearly shows which customer purchased which product, when, in what quantity, and at what price.

This section is important for analyzing sales profitability. For example:

* Which sales were more profitable
* Which products generate low profit
* How discounts and costs affect profit
* Sales activity in a specific time period.

The first part of the table has the following columns:

* **Customer** — person or organization who purchased the product.
* **Product** — name of the sold product.
* **Category** — product group.
* **Unit of Measure** — unit in which the product is sold (kg, liter, piece, etc.).
* **Sales Number** — sales document number (can be clicked to open the document).
* **Status** — status of the sales process (Completed, etc.).
* **Quantity** — volume of sold product.
* **Price** — unit price.
* **Discount** — discount amount applied in the sale.
* **Tax Included / Tax Added** — tax indicators.
* **Total Price** — total sales amount of the sold product.
* **Total Cost** — total cost of sold products.
* **Gross Profit** — net profit from this sale.
* **Time Sold** — date and time the sale was made.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (228).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



</details>

<summary>Customer Sales</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (229).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Customer Sales** page is designed to show all sales made for the selected customer.

When entering this report, no data appears initially. The report is generated only **after a customer is selected**. The selected customer's sales are displayed based on active filters (date, organization, and other parameters).

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (230).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Report Purpose**

This page enables identification of:

* Customer's total purchase volume
* Which products they purchased most
* In which period they made active purchases
* Volume of discounts given to the customer

This report is an important tool for analyzing customer relationships, identifying loyal customers, and forming individual sales strategies.



</details>

<summary>Sales by Customer</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (240).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This report shows all sales by customer segment. That is, in each sales operation, which customer made the purchase, sales date, and payment details are visible. Through this report, it's possible to analyze which customer paid when and by what method.

The table displays the following information:

* **Customer** — name of the customer who made the sale
* **Date** — time when the sale was made
* **Price** — total value of the sale
* **Cash** — amount paid by cash
* **Card** — payment by bank card
* **Terminal** — payment via POS terminal
* **Money Transfer** — payment via bank transfer
* **Debt** — amount recorded as debt
* **Click** — payment via Click
* **From Balance** — amount paid from customer balance
* **Total Payment** — total amount paid
* **Note** — additional comment on the sale

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (237).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

When the **expand button on the left side of each sales row is pressed**, the list of products sold in that sale is also displayed. The following information appears:

* **Name** — product name
* **Quantity** — number of products sold
* **Price** — price per unit of product
* **Total Price** — total amount for the product

Through this feature, it's possible to see in detail exactly which products were sold in each customer sale.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (238).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<summary>Non-Selling Products Report</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (231).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The non-selling products report allows viewing products that sold poorly or didn't sell in the selected period. The report shows product name, category, unit of measure, quantity sold, sales amount, and current warehouse stock. With this information, it's possible to identify which products are moving slowly and make decisions about discounts, promotions, or reordering for them.

**Product Name** — name of the product.\
**Category** — shows which category or type the product belongs to.\
**Unit of Measure** — indicates in what unit the product is counted (for example: piece, kg, liter).\
**Quantity** — quantity of product sold in the selected period.\
**Amount** — total sales amount received from this product sale.\
**Current Product Balance** — quantity of product currently remaining in the warehouse.

At the bottom of the table is a **Total** row showing the total quantity and total sales amount for all products by the selected filter.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (232).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

</details>

<summary>Agent Report</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (234).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Agents Report** enables analysis of agents (salespeople or representatives) who participated in the sales process. The report shows information about each agent's sales, returned sales, discounts, and revenues. With this information, it's possible to assess which agent is making more sales, their effectiveness, and overall sales results.

**Agent** — name of the agent who participated in the sale.\
**Sales** — total sales amount made through the agent.\
**Returned** — amount of returned products.\
**Discount** — total amount of discounts given during sales.\
**Revenue** — final revenue taking into account returns and discounts.\
**Sold** — number of sales operations made.\
**Quantity Sold** — total quantity of products sold.\
**Quantity Returned** — number of returned products.\
**Average Daily Sales** — agent's average sales amount per day.\
**Number of New Customers** — number of new customers added to the system through this agent.\
**Gross Profit** — total revenue indicator received through the agent.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (233).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

</details>

<details>

<summary>Customer Payments</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (235).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Customer Payments** report displays information about all payments and contracts related to customers. Through this report, it's possible to track payments for products sold on credit, monitor customer debts, and payments made.

The top of the report displays the following indicators:

* **Total Contracts** — number of all contracts in the system and their total amount
* **Initial Payments** — initial payments made when the contract was signed
* **Overdue Payments** — debts whose payment deadline has passed but are still unpaid
* **Payments** — total amount of all payments made by customers
* **Expected Payments** — amounts whose payment deadline hasn't come yet but should be paid in the future
* **Completed Contracts** — contracts that have been fully paid and closed

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (236).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The bottom table section shows detailed information for each contract:

* **Number** — contract or sales number
* **Customer** — customer making the payment
* **Amount Due** — total amount to be paid
* **Paid** — amount paid so far
* **Initial Payment** — payment made initially
* **Responsible Person** — employee responsible for the sale or contract
* **Date** — contract or sales date

This report enables management of not only products sold on credit but all customer payments. Even if a sale is not on credit, it's reflected in the **contract**, **initial payment**, **payments**, and **completed contracts** sections. Through this, it's possible to control all customer payments in one place.

</details>
