<summary>Sales by Customer</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (136).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Sales by Customer** report displays all sales broken down by customer. This report allows you to analyze which customer made how many sales, returns, and revenue metrics.

The table shows the following data:

* **Customer** — name of the customer who made the purchase
* **Category** — customer type or category the customer belongs to
* **Sales** — total sales amount made by the customer
* **Returned** — amount of products returned by the customer
* **Discount** — total discounts given to the customer
* **Tax Included** — tax amount included in the sale
* **Tax Added** — additional tax amount added to the sale
* **Revenue** — net income from sales
* **Gross Profit** — profit after deducting product cost

At the bottom of the table:

* **Total** — overall results by selected filters
* **Forecast** — forecast metrics calculated by the system

Using this report, you can identify top-selling customers, analyze revenue by customer, and improve sales strategy.

<summary>Customer Activity</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (138).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Customer Activity** report displays information about customer activity in the sales process. This report allows you to analyze which customer has been making purchases since when, when their last sale was, and how many purchases they have made.

The table shows the following data:

* **Full Name** — customer's full name
* **Phone Number** — customer's contact number
* **Category** — category the customer belongs to
* **First Sale** — date of the first purchase made by the customer
* **Last Sale** — date of the customer's most recent purchase
* **Number of Purchases** — total number of purchases made by the customer
* **Average Daily Sales** — customer's average daily sales metric

Using this report, you can identify active customers, find customers who haven't made purchases in a long time, and improve customer engagement strategy.

<summary>Cashback Turnover Report</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (139).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Cashback Turnover Report** displays detailed information about cashback funds accumulated and used by customers. This report allows you to analyze the cashback system's performance, how much cashback customers have accumulated, and how much has been used.

The table shows the following data:

* **Name** — customer name or identifier
* **Phone Number** — customer's contact number
* **Initial Cashback** — cashback amount initially available in the customer's account
* **Initial Cashback (breakdown)** — initial cashback shown in other currency
* **Accumulated Cashback** — cashback amount accumulated through sales
* **Accumulated Cashback (breakdown)** — accumulated cashback in other currency
* **Used Cashback** — cashback amount used by the customer during purchases
* **Used Cashback (breakdown)** — used cashback in other currency
* **Final Cashback** — cashback remaining in the customer's balance after all transactions

**Example:**

If a customer earns **1000 som cashback** through a sale and then uses that cashback in the next purchase, the report will look as follows:

* **Initial Cashback:** 0 som
* **Accumulated Cashback:** 1000 som
* **Used Cashback:** 1000 som
* **Final Cashback:** 0 som

<summary>Customer ABC XYZ Analysis</summary>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (141).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Customer ABC–XYZ Analysis** report segments customers by sales volume and purchase stability. This analysis helps the business identify the most important customers, properly plan sales strategy, and optimize customer engagement.



The report evaluates customers using two main methods: **ABC analysis** and **XYZ analysis**.

**ABC analysis** divides customers into groups based on revenue or sales volume:

* **A** — customers generating the highest revenue
* **B** — customers generating medium revenue
* **C** — customers generating relatively low revenue

**XYZ analysis** shows the stability of customer purchases:

* **X** — customers making regular and stable purchases
* **Y** — customers with medium stability
* **Z** — customers making irregular or infrequent purchases



As a result of these two analyses, customers are divided into **segments** (for example: **AX, BY, CZ** and others). These segments help shape marketing, discount policies, and customer engagement strategies.

The table displays the following data:

* **Customer** — customer name
* **Revenue** — total income from the customer
* **Profit** — net profit from customer sales
* **ABC Category** — customer's group by sales volume
* **XYZ Category** — customer's purchase stability level
* **Segment** — overall combination of ABC and XYZ analysis
* **Average Monthly** — customer's average monthly sales volume
* **Standard Deviation** — level of variability in sales volume
* **Coefficient of Variation** — shows how stable or variable sales are
* **Active Months** — number of months the customer made purchases
