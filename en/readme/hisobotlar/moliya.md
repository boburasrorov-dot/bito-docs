# Finance



### Income-Expense Report

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (95).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This report shows the cash flow in the enterprise. Through the report, you can analyze all **income (revenue)** and **expenses (costs)** carried out during the selected date range. Using the chart, cash movement is displayed by day, and you can see how much income or expense came from which source.

The report consists of two sections: **Income** and **Expenses**.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (96).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Income** section shows all funds received in the cash register or account during the selected date range.

The chart displays income by day, showing how much revenue was received each day.

On the right side, income sources and their total amounts are displayed.

For example:\
**Sales (product sales)** — total revenue received through product sales.



<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (97).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

The **Expenses** section shows all costs incurred during the selected date range.

The chart shows the distribution of expenses by day.

On the right side, expense types and their amounts are displayed.

For example:\
**Office services** — expenses related to office operations.



**Business Benefits**

Using this report:

* you can control cash flow in the enterprise
* you can compare and analyze revenue and expenses
* you can identify which source brings more revenue
* you can see which types of expenses are higher
* you can assess the financial condition of the business





### Cash Turnover Report

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (98).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

This report shows cash turnover by cash registers. Through the report, you can analyze funds received in the cash register (income), funds withdrawn from the cash register (expenses), as well as the balance at the beginning and end of the period during the selected date range. The report allows full control of cash movement for each cash register.

The table shows the overall cash register indicators. Additionally, by expanding the cash register row, you can view cash movement **by day**.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (99).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (100).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**The report table contains the following columns:**

**№** — row number in the table.

**Cash Register** — cash register name.

**Base Currency** — the main currency in which the cash register operates.

**Opening Balance (Expanded)** — the balance in the cash register at the beginning of the period is shown by different currencies.

**Opening Balance** — total cash balance in the cash register at the beginning of the period.

**Income (Expanded)** — funds received in the cash register are shown by currencies.

**Income** — total funds received in the cash register.

**Expenses (Expanded)** — funds withdrawn from the cash register are shown by currencies.

**Expenses** — total funds withdrawn from the cash register.

**Net Turnover (Expanded)** — the difference between income and expenses is shown by currencies.

**Net Turnover** — the difference between funds received and withdrawn from the cash register.

**Closing Balance (Expanded)** — the balance at the end of the period is shown by currencies.

**Closing Balance** — total cash balance remaining in the cash register at the end of the period.



**Business Benefits**

Using this report:

* you can control cash turnover by cash registers
* you can compare and analyze income and expenses
* you can see the exact cash balance in the cash register
* you can track cash movement by day
* you can effectively manage the enterprise's financial operations

### Cash Session

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (115).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Cash Session** — this is the work period during the time interval when the cash register was opened and closed by the cashier. During the session, the cashier makes sales, accepts payments, and cash movements are carried out. When the session is closed, the system calculates the opening balance, entered funds, and closing balance.

This report shows data on cash sessions. Through the report, you can see which cashier opened the cash register when, when it was closed, cash balances, and session closing amounts. This report helps control cash operations and analyze cashier activities.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (124).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**The report table contains the following columns**

**№** — row number in the table.

**Session ID** — identification number of each cash session in the system.

**Opened Time** — time when the cash session started.

**Closed Time** — time when the cash session was closed.

**Cashier** — employee who worked with the cash register.

**Cash Register** — shows through which cash register or cash box the session was conducted.

**Opening Balance** — amount of cash available in the cash register at the beginning of the session.

**Closing Amount** — total amount of cash in the cash register when the session was closed.

**Total** — total cash movement or closing balance during the session.



At the top of the report, overall statistics by sessions are displayed:

**Total** — number of all cash sessions in the system.

**Opened** — number of currently open cash sessions.

**Closed** — number of closed cash sessions.

**Flagged** — sessions where problems or differences were identified by the system.

**Total Difference** — total cash difference identified across all sessions.



Using this report:

* you can control cashiers' work
* you can see cash movements in each cash session
* you can identify cash differences when closing the cash register
* you can control cash discipline
* auditing cash operations becomes easier







### ABC Analysis

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (116).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**What is ABC Analysis?**

ABC analysis — is a method of dividing products into three groups according to sales volume or revenue.

In this method, products are divided into the following categories:

Category A — the most important products. They provide a large portion of sales, but their number is small.

Category B — products with average sales volume.

Category C — products that have little impact on sales. They may be numerous, but their contribution to sales is small.

ABC analysis helps businesses identify which products are most important.

This report analyzes products based on sales indicators. Through the report, each product's sold quantity, sales amount, discount, tax, revenue, cost, and gross profit are displayed. Based on this data, the system categorizes products into A, B, or C categories.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (125).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**The report table contains the following columns**

**№** — row number in the table.

**Product** — product name.

**Unit of Measure** — product unit of measure (pieces, kilograms, etc.).

**Sold Quantity** — quantity of product sold during the selected period.

**Sales** — total sales amount from the product.

**Discount** — discount amount applied to the product.

**Added Tax** — tax added during sales.

**Input Tax** — tax related to purchase or cost.

**Revenue** — net revenue received from the product.

**Cost** — production or purchase cost of the product.

**Gross Profit** — difference between sales revenue and cost.

**Share** — the product's contribution share to total sales.

**Type** — shows which ABC category the product belongs to.



**Adjusting ABC Boundaries through Filter**

In the report, **you can change A, B, and C category boundaries through the filter.**

For example:

* **A — 20%**
* **B — 60%**
* **C — 20%**

These values mean that:

* products providing the highest **20% of sales — Category A**
* products providing the next **60% of sales — Category B**
* remaining **20% of products — Category C**

Users can change these percentages through the filter and customize the analysis to their business.



**Business Benefits**

Using this report:

* you can identify products that bring the most revenue
* you can see low-selling products
* you can optimize warehouse and sales strategy
* you can focus more on the most important products
* you can analyze product assortment
