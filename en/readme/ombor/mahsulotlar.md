# Products



### Creating Products

The Products section enables the creation, editing, and management of all warehouse products. This section serves to store basic product information and link them with warehouse, sales, and other processes.

The user accesses the **Warehouse → Products** section from the left menu and clicks the **"Create"** button to add a new product.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (371).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

When creating a product, the following information is entered:

* **Name** — product name is entered
* **Category** — the appropriate product category is selected
* **Unit of Measurement** — product measurement type (piece, kg, liter, etc.)
* **Package Type** — product packaging type is selected
* **Quantity per Package** — number of products in one package
* **Package Barcode** — separate barcode for the package
* **SKU** — unique product identification code
* **Barcode** — main product barcode
* **Supplier** — product supplier
* **Warehouse Location** — product placement (rack, sector, etc.)
* **Notes** — additional information (optional)
* **File Upload** — attach relevant file to the product

Inventory settings:

* **Product / Semi-finished Product / Raw Material** — product type is specified, and the product is classified according to the selected option
* **Variant Product** — use as a variant product
* **Serialized Product** — for special identification
* **Expiry Date** — maintain expiration tracking

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (373).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Product Composition (additional settings):

In this section, parameters related to warehouse and sales can be configured:

* **Organization** — which organizations the product belongs to
* **Maximum Quantity** — maximum inventory threshold
* **Standard Days** — when the product runs out and an order is placed, how many days it takes for the product to arrive (for analytics)
* **Yellow Line** — warning level
* **Red Line** — critical minimum level
* **Available for Sale** — release product for sale
* **Retail** — retail price
* **Wholesale** — wholesale price
* **Other prices can be created by the user**

Through this section, products are fully managed and serve as the foundation for subsequent processes (receipts, issues, inventory, sales).





### Creating Variant Products

If a product is available in multiple variants (e.g., color, size, etc.), they can be managed through variants within a single product rather than as separate products.

To do this, the **"Variant Product"** toggle switch is enabled in the product creation window. When this function is enabled, an additional **"Variant Product"** window opens in the system.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (375).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

In this section, product variants are entered as follows:

* **Variant** — the product variant type is selected (e.g., color, size)
* **Attribute** — values corresponding to the selected variant are entered (e.g., white, black)

After entering the variants, the **"Add Products"** button is pressed. The system then automatically forms the **"Product Composition"** section.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (376).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>



In the Product Composition section:

* a separate product (SKU) is created for each variant
* **SKU** — entered separately for each variant or
* generated by the system through **Automatic Creation**

As a result, products automatically multiply according to the number of entered attributes.

For example:\
If the **color** variant is selected for the "Phone" product and **red** and **black** are entered as attributes, the system automatically creates 2 products:

* Phone / red
* Phone / black

This approach enables systematic product management, individual accounting for each variant, and simplifies sales processes.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (377).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (379).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

Creating products through variants enables more structured and efficient product management in the system.

Using this function achieves the following:

* **Organized Product Management** — different variants of the same product (color, size, etc.) are stored within a single product, preventing system clutter
* **Individual Control of Each Variant** — separate SKU, price, and inventory can be maintained for each variant
* **Improved Warehouse Accounting** — for example, "red" and "black" products are accounted for separately, clearly showing which has more or less stock
* **Simplified Sales Process** — the exact variant is selected during sale, reducing errors
* **Automatic Product Creation** — the system automatically generates multiple products based on variant attributes, eliminating manual creation
* **Flexibility** — adding or modifying new variants is easy, making system expansion convenient

As a result, product management through variants reaches a professional level, and warehouse and sales processes operate much more accurately and quickly.





### Creating Serialized Products

If the **"Serialized Product"** toggle switch is enabled when creating a product, each unit of this product is tracked with a **unique identification (serial number)**.

In this function, the product is managed not by simple quantity, but **per individual unit**.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (381).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

As a result, the following is implemented in the system:

* a separate **unique code (serial number, IMEI, etc.)** is assigned to each product
* product movement (receipt, issue, inventory, sale) is tracked through this specific identifier
* identical products do not get mixed up, each is controlled separately

How the process works:

During inventory, receipt, or sales:

* the system requires entering **a separate identifier for each unit** rather than the product quantity
* the user manually enters or uploads unique codes for each product
* the system saves these identifiers and identifies the exact product in subsequent operations

For example:\
If an "iPhone" product is created as a serialized product, when 10 units are received into the warehouse, a separate IMEI number is entered for each iPhone. Later during sale or issue, the exact IMEI that was sold is clearly visible in the system.

Benefits:

* accurate control of valuable and important products
* prevention of loss and confusion
* easy management of warranty and service processes
* tracking the history of each product (where it came from, when it was sold)

This functionality is typically applied for **electronics stores, electronics sales, and products that work with serial numbers**.





### Creating Products with Expiry Dates

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (387).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If the **"Expiry Date"** toggle switch is enabled when creating a product, working with expiration dates is enabled for this product.

This means the product is managed not by simple quantity, but **by expiry date**.

This functionality is primarily used for food, pharmaceuticals, and perishable products.

How the process works:

* **During Purchase (Receipt)**\
  when the product is received into the warehouse, an **expiry date is entered** for each product
* **During Warehouse Storage**\
  products are stored in separate batches according to expiry date
* **During Sales**\
  when selling the product, the user selects **which expiry date the product being sold has**

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (388).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (389).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

As a result, the following is ensured in the system:

* products are accurately controlled by date
* the ability to track products approaching expiration appears
* errors when working with outdated or expired products are reduced
* applying the FIFO (first in — first out) principle becomes easier

For example:\
If the expiry date is enabled for the "Kefir" product, a date is entered for each batch when received into the warehouse (e.g., 23.03.2026). During sale, the product is selected and sold according to this specific date.

This plays an important role in quality control and reducing losses.
