# Working with Logistics Orders



### Orders

**Orders (Logistics Department)** serves to manage delivery-related orders created during the sales process. If customer information or delivery address was entered during the sale, the system automatically creates an order in the logistics department based on this information. This organically links sales and delivery processes, eliminates the need for manual re-entry, and reduces the likelihood of errors.

Access **Logistics → Orders** section where all delivery orders are displayed.

This section operates in two view modes: **table** and **kanban**.

In table view, all orders are displayed in list format.

**Table columns:**

* **Created time** – order creation date and time
* **Modified time** – last modification time
* **Number** – order identifier
* **Status** – order status (New, Completed, etc.)
* **Base type** – order source (e.g.: Sales)
* **Custom status** – additional status (e.g.: new)
* **Total quantity** – number of products in the order
* **Organization** – branch or organization to which the order belongs
* **Recipient** – person receiving the order (customer)
* **Total additional amount** – additional expenses (delivery cost, etc.)
* **Delivered time** – actual delivery time
* **Comment** – additional information
* **Delivery date** – scheduled delivery date

In kanban view, orders are displayed as cards divided by status (e.g.: **New, Completed, Cancelled**). Through this view, users can quickly manage order statuses visually and move them from one status to another.

This section is directly linked to sales, and every delivery order created in sales is reflected here. Therefore, logistics staff can track and manage all delivery processes solely through this section. This centralizes the process, prevents orders from being lost, and enables systematic control of deliveries.
