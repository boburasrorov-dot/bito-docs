# Employees

<details>

<summary>Adding an Employee</summary>

The **Employees section** serves to manage all employees in the system and maintain their information. Through this section, you can register employees, associate them with their position, department, and system role. Additionally, granting employees access to the system or storing them only as information is also managed here.

Access the **HR → Employees** section and add a new employee through the **"Create"** button.

In this section, employees are displayed in a table view.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (468).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Table columns:**

* **Personnel number** – employee's internal identification number
* **Full name** – employee's first and last name
* **Hire date** – date of employment
* **Phone number** – contact number (used as login)
* **Employee type** – employee category (for example: paid or free user)

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (473).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

When adding a new employee, all basic information is entered through the form.

* **Name** – employee name
* **Phone number** – used as login for system access
* **Hire date** – work start date
* **Address** – residential address
* **Note** – additional information
* **Photo** – employee photo
* **Gender, last name, patronymic** – personal information
* **Date of birth** – birth date
* **Tax ID** – tax identification number
* **Email / Telegram** – additional contact information
* **Status** – indicates whether the employee is active or inactive
* **Courier** – marked if the employee is a courier

When creating an employee, there are concepts of **free user** and **paid user**.

If an employee is designated as a **free user**, they cannot access the system and do not participate in any processes. Such employees are stored only as information (for example, for record-keeping or history purposes).

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (474).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

If an employee is a **paid user**:

* **Phone number** – used as login
* **PIN (6 digits)** – used for mobile app access
* **Password (minimum 8 characters)** – used for web system access

PIN and password are only provided to paid users, and through them, the employee can work in various modules of the system.

By assigning a position and permissions to an employee, their system rights are defined. Therefore, each employee works only with the sections and functions permitted to them.

Through this section, it is possible to manage all company employees centrally, control their roles, and ensure system security.

<summary>Employee Card (created employee window)</summary>

The **Employee Card (created employee window)** serves to view all information about an employee, track their balance, and manage financial and system operations related to them. In this window, the employee's basic information, permissions, and all related actions are collected in one place.

This page opens when clicking on the desired employee from the employee list.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (475).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

On the left side, the employee's basic information is displayed:

* **Name and photo** – employee identification
* **Balance** – financial balance associated with the employee
* **Hire date** – work start date
* **Phone number** – used for contact and login
* **Permissions** – roles assigned to the employee and their system rights (for example: salesperson, accountant, etc.)

At the bottom:

* **Delete** – remove the employee from the system
* **Edit** – modify employee information

On the right side, financial and operational information related to the employee is displayed.

In the top menu, the following sections are available:

* **Mutual settlements report** – all income-expense operations related to the employee
* **Balance** – overall balance view
* **Invoice** – documents related to the employee
* **Payment history** – completed payments
* **Notes** – additional records
* **Audit history** – history of changes in the system

In the **Mutual settlements report** section:

* **Income** – funds received by the employee
* **Expense** – funds used or given by the employee

Table columns:

* **Time** – operation date
* **Operation** – type of operation performed
* **Note** – additional information
* **Debit / Credit** – financial transaction
* **Balance** – balance after the operation

In this window, the user can fully control all financial transactions related to the employee. Therefore, this page is important for maintaining settlements with employees, identifying debts or overpayments, and tracking the overall financial situation.
