# Departments

<details open>

<summary>Departments</summary>

**The Departments section** serves to form the structure within the company and properly distribute employees. Through this section, departments within the organization (for example: Sales, Finance, Logistics, etc.) are created and connected to each other in a hierarchical manner. This simplifies employee management and creates an orderly structure in the system.

Navigate to **HR → Departments** section and add a new department through the **"Create"** button

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (476).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

In this section, all departments are displayed in table view.

**Table columns:**

* **Name** – department name
* **Parent Department** – which main department this department belongs to

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (477).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

When creating a new department, the following information is entered:

* **Name** – department name (required)
* **Parent Department** – if the department is part of another department, it is selected here

Additionally, in the department creation window, existing employees can be assigned to this department:

* Each employee is selected from the list
* A separate employee can be designated as **Manager (head)**

Through this:

* employees are assigned to each department
* a responsible person (manager) is designated within the department

Through this section, the hierarchy (structure) within the company is formed. As a result:

* it becomes clear which department employees belong to
* managing roles and permissions becomes easier
* management in large organizations becomes systematic

If a parent department is selected, the system automatically creates a connection between departments (parent-child structure). This enables the creation of a multi-level organizational structure.
