# KPI



### Setting and Viewing KPI

The **KPI section** is used to evaluate employee performance and calculate bonuses (incentives) for them. Through this section, KPIs are set for a specific period and the system automatically calculates employee results based on established criteria. This helps motivate employees and increase business metrics.

Access **HR → KPI** section and add a new KPI through the **"Create"** button.

All KPIs are displayed in this section in table view.

**Table columns:**

* **Organization** – which branch or organization the KPI belongs to
* **Bonus type** – what type of bonus is given (percentage, amount, etc.)
* **Period** – KPI period (daily, weekly, monthly)
* **Calculation method** – how the KPI is calculated (for example: threshold or progressive)
* **Status** – active or inactive
* **Start date / End date** – KPI validity period

When creating a new KPI, the following main settings are entered:

* **Employees** – who the KPI applies to
* **Period** – time interval for KPI calculation (for example: week)
* **Date** – start date
* **Status** – active or inactive
* **Calculation method** – KPI calculation logic (for example: progressive or threshold-based)

**KPI calculation conditions:**

* **Target source** – what the KPI is based on:
  * Revenue
  * Amount paid
  * Number of sales
  * Average check and others
* **Range (From – To)** – KPI limits are set
* Multiple conditions can be added (**+ Add condition**)

**Bonus settings:**

* **Bonus type**:
  * Revenue percentage
  * Payment percentage
  * Per each sale
  * Fixed amount and others
* **Bonus value** – entered as percentage or amount

After the KPI is created, it appears in the list and a detailed page opens when clicked.

On this page:

* Employee assigned to the KPI
* KPI conditions
* Bonus settings
* and most importantly, **results (KPI achievement by employee)** are displayed

That is, the system automatically calculates the employee's result and outputs how much bonus they received.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (485).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

#### Example:

If the following KPI is set:

* **Target source:** Revenue
* **Range:** 0 – 10,000,000 UZS
* **Bonus type:** 10% of revenue

If an employee (for example: Bobur) makes **8,000,000 UZS in sales** in one week:

* The KPI is considered achieved
* The system automatically calculates:\
  👉 8,000,000 × 10% = **800,000 UZS bonus**

As a result, this amount is displayed per employee within the KPI.
